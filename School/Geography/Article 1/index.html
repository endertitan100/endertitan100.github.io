Why was Hurricane Ian a "bad" Hurricane

Hurricane Ian was the fifth strongest storm to make landfall in the U.S. It was a large destructive hurricane that hit western Cuba and southern U.S. on 28th September 2022. The catagory 4 hurricane claimed more than 430 lives and some estimates have calculated the damage at $55bn.

Major Impacts

  • At least 430 death and thousands injured
  • 2.6 million homes left without power
  • $41 billion - $70 billion in damage caused
  • Max wind speed of 249 km/h or 154.7 mph
  • At least 40,600 people were displaced

Let's do some comparison

We will compare Hurricane Ian to Hurricane Fiona that hit Puerto Rico

Deaths

Hurricane Ian killed at least 106 people wheras Hurricane Fiona killed 31 people

Estimated Cost

Hurricane Fiona is expected to cost > $2.68 billion to repair. Hurricane Ian on the other hand is expected to cost $55 billion.

Location

Hurricane Fiona made landfall in Nova Scotia and New Foundland. Hurricane Ian made landfall in in Georgetown, South Carolina.
